Study Guide: Real Numbers (Class 10)

1. Euclid's Division Lemma (EDL)

Given positive integers a and b, there exist unique integers q (quotient) and r (remainder) such that:

a = bq + r, where 0 ≤ r < b

Application: Euclid's Division Algorithm is used to find the HCF (Highest Common Factor) of two positive integers.

Example: Find HCF of 42 and 455. 455 = 42 × 10 + 35. Then 42 = 35 × 1 + 7. Then 35 = 7 × 5 + 0. HCF is 7.

2. Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic (FTA)

Every composite number can be expressed (factorised) as a product of primes, and this factorisation is unique, apart from the order in which the prime factors occur.

Composite Number = p₁ × p₂ × p₃ × ...

Key Relation for HCF and LCM:

HCF(a, b) × LCM(a, b) = a × b

Application (Ending with 0): A number x ends with the digit 0 only if its prime factorization contains both 2 and 5.

3. Revisiting Irrational Numbers

The method used is Proof by Contradiction.

  • Type 1: Proving √p is Irrational (where p is prime):

    Assume √p = a/b (coprime). Show that p divides both a and b, which contradicts the coprime assumption.

  • Type 2: Proving a+b√p is Irrational:

    Assume a+b√p is rational. Isolate √p. Since a, b, and the assumed number are rational, √p would be rational, which is a known contradiction.

4. Decimal Expansions (Rational Numbers)

A rational number p/q (where HCF(p, q) = 1) has a decimal expansion that:

  • Terminates if the prime factorization of the denominator q is of the form:
    q = 2ⁿ × 5ᵐ, where n, m ≥ 0 are integers
  • Is Non-terminating Repeating (Recurring) if the prime factorization of the denominator q is NOT of the form 2ⁿ × 5ᵐ (i.e., it has a prime factor other than 2 or 5).

Example: 13/3125 terminates because 3125 = 5⁵. 17/8 terminates because 8 = 2³. 64/455 is non-terminating repeating because 455 = 5 × 7 × 13.
