Heart, Blood, and Vessels: The Body's Transport Network

I. Components & Purpose

What is Circulation?

The Circulatory System is a group of organs (Heart, Blood, Blood Vessels) that work together to transport essential substances throughout the body.

The Purpose

  • Supply Oxygen, Food, and Water to every body part.
  • Collect Carbon Dioxide and other Waste for removal.
  • Deliver Nutrients from the digestive system.

Blood: The Transport Medium

Blood is a red liquid that flows through the vessels. It carries Oxygen to tissues and collects Carbon Dioxide and other waste products (like from the liver).

II. The Heart: The Pumping Machine

Structure and Function

  • Location: Lies between the lungs, slightly toward the left side of the chest.
  • Chambers: Consists of four chambers: two upper Atria and two lower Ventricles.
  • Valves (Walls): Allow blood to flow in one direction only.
  • Action: It continuously contracts and relaxes like a pumping machine.
  • Heart Beat & Pulse: Contraction produces the heartbeat. The wavy movement in arteries caused by pumping is the pulse.

III. Blood Vessels: The Tubes

There are three main types of thin tubes forming the network throughout the body:

ARTERIES

The High-Pressure Path

  • Direction: Heart → Rest of Body.
  • Blood Type: Carry Oxygenated Blood.
  • Feature: Have thick, elastic walls (for pressure).

VEINS

The Low-Pressure Return

  • Direction: Body → Heart.
  • Blood Type: Carry Deoxygenated Blood.
  • Feature: Have thinner walls and often contain valves.

CAPILLARIES

The Exchange Zone

  • Function: Site of gas and nutrient exchange.
  • Feature: Thinnest vessels, forming dense networks.
  • Connection: Link Arteries to Veins.

IV. The Circulation Cycle

1. Body sends Deoxygenated Blood to the Heart (via Veins)
2. Heart pumps Deoxygenated Blood to the Lungs
3. Lungs remove CO2 and add O2 (Blood becomes Oxygenated)
4. Oxygenated Blood returns to the Heart
5. Heart pumps Oxygenated Blood to all parts of the Body (via Arteries)

V. Maintaining a Healthy Heart

Dietary Habits

  • Eat a balanced diet.
  • Avoid or limit fatty and sugary foods, as they are harmful for heart health.

Exercise and Well-being

  • Engage in regular exercise (e.g., jogging, brisk walking).
  • Exercise pumps more blood throughout the body.
  • Yoga and deep breathing help the respiratory system, which in turn aids the heart's function.
