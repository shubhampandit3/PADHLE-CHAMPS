Poem Analysis: "Leisure"

By W. H. Davies | A Critique of Modern Rushed Life

The Complete Poem

What is this life if, full of care,
We have no time to stand and stare.

No time to stand beneath the boughs,
And stare as long as sheep or cows.

No time to see, when woods we pass,
Where squirrels hide their nuts in grass.

No time to see, in broad daylight,
Streams full of stars, like skies at night.

No time to turn at Beauty's glance,
And watch her feet, how they can dance.

No time to wait till her mouth can,
Enrich that smile her eyes began.

A poor life this if, full of care,
We have no time to stand and stare.

I. The Poet and Core Concept

The Author

The poem "Leisure" was written by W. H. Davies.

Included in English Literature curriculum (English 2).

Central Idea

The poet argues that modern life is too busy and full of worries. People do not pause to enjoy the beauty of nature. A life without leisure is empty, poor, and incomplete.

II. Line-by-Line Breakdown & Word Meanings

"Full of Care"

Life becomes meaningless if always busy with worries, without time to pause.

  • Care: worries, responsibilities
  • Stand and stare: to calmly pause

"Sheep or Cows"

Animals calmly rest under trees, unlike rushing humans.

  • Boughs: branches of a tree
  • Sheep/cows: symbols of peace

"Squirrels Hide"

We miss small wonders like squirrels hiding nuts when rushing through life.

"Streams full of stars"

Streams sparkle in sunlight like night stars, but people fail to notice.

"Beauty’s Glance"

Beauty is personified as a woman; humans are too busy to admire her charm.

"Her Smile and Eyes"

We lack time to enjoy simple joys—like a smile beginning in the eyes.

"A Poor Life"

The poem concludes: A life without leisure and reflection is wasted.

III. Q&A Review

30 questions based on the poem and analysis.

Q1. Who wrote the poem "Leisure"?

A. W. H. Davies.

Q2. What is the central theme of the poem?

A. Importance of leisure and enjoying nature.

Q3. What does “full of care” mean?

A. Life filled with worries and duties.

Q4. What is meant by “stand and stare”?

A. To pause and admire surroundings.

Q5. Which animals are used for comparison?

A. Sheep and cows.

Q6. Why are sheep and cows mentioned?

A. They calmly enjoy leisure unlike humans.

Q7. What do squirrels do in the poem?

A. Hide nuts in grass.

Q8. What lesson do squirrels teach?

A. Beauty lies in small unnoticed details.

Q9. What are “streams full of stars”?

A. Sparkling water reflecting light.

Q10. What is compared to stars in daylight?

A. Sparkles in flowing streams.

Q11. What does “Beauty’s glance” symbolize?

A. Nature’s beauty personified as a woman.

Q12. What do “dancing feet” represent?

A. Joyful and graceful beauty of nature.

Q13. What does the poet miss about Beauty?

A. Her smile, eyes, and graceful dance.

Q14. What does “her eyes began” suggest?

A. A smile that starts in the eyes.

Q15. What is the tone of the poem?

A. Regretful and reflective.

Q16. What does the last stanza call life without leisure?

A. A poor life.

Q17. Why is life called poor without leisure?

A. Because we miss beauty and joy.

Q18. What message does the poet want to give?

A. We must slow down and admire nature.

Q19. How many couplets are in the poem?

A. 7 couplets (14 lines).

Q20. What is meant by “boughs”?

A. Branches of a tree.

Q21. What does the word “woods” mean?

A. Forest or group of trees.

Q22. How are duties described in the poem?

A. As “care” that fills life with worries.

Q23. What do streams represent in the poem?

A. Natural wonders often ignored.

Q24. What is the poet criticizing?

A. The rushed and busy modern lifestyle.

Q25. What literary device is used in “streams full of stars”?

A. Metaphor.

Q26. What is the rhyme scheme of the poem?

A. AA couplets.

Q27. What is the structure of the poem?

A. 14 lines, rhyming couplets.

Q28. What feeling does the poet want to awaken?

A. Awareness of leisure’s importance.

Q29. What is the ultimate conclusion of the poem?

A. Life is wasted without time for nature.

Q30. In one phrase, what is the poem about?

A. The value of leisure.
